🎯 What Is This Framework?

A comprehensive system designed for IT Managers who need to oversee external contractors (Networking Engineers & Sales Managers), create professional daily/weekly/monthly reports, and successfully complete their probation period to secure a permanent position.

👥 The 3 People in Your System

🔧 Networking Engineer (NE)

External contractor - Building network infrastructure

Collect Daily:

  • Task list (planned vs actual)
  • Photos of equipment installed
  • Configuration screenshots
  • Cable labeling docs
  • Issues & resolutions
  • Materials used
  • Hours worked
  • Tomorrow's plan

💼 Sales Manager (SM)

External contractor - Client acquisition & pipeline

Collect Daily:

  • Call log (count + contacts)
  • Meeting notes
  • Proposals sent (value)
  • Follow-up status
  • Deals won/lost
  • Pipeline total value
  • CRM screenshots

⭐ You - IT Manager

Internal - Observing, documenting, reporting

Your Job:

  • Document every step they take
  • Photo/screenshot evidence
  • Quality checks vs scope
  • Risk identification
  • Daily CEO reports
  • Weekly summaries
  • Monthly presentations
  • Build technical knowledge

🎯 Probation Period Success Tips

🏆 Goal: Make the CEO feel they can see everything without being on-site. Do this consistently for 90 days = permanent position.

The 8 Rules to Get Permanent

1. Always Send Report

Even if news is bad. Even if day was quiet. CEO trusts truth, not perfection.

Example: "Today was primarily planning. No major issues. On track for tomorrow."

2. Add Recommendation Daily

One suggestion shows leadership thinking ahead.

Example: "Suggest ordering CAT6 tools by Thursday to avoid cabling delay."

3. Build Live Tracker

Google Sheet shared with CEO. Shows you're organized. Update every morning.

4. Document with Photos

5 photos daily. Month-end: send before/after comparison. Visual proof beats words.

5. Learn One Thing Daily

Ask NE to explain one topic. After 30 days = 30 new skills documented.

6. Ask for Feedback (Week 2)

"Is my report format working? Anything to add/change?" Shows maturity.

7. Never Skip a Report

Not even one day. Consistency over 90 days converts probation to permanent.

8. Present Monthly Summary

Request 20-min meeting to walk CEO through month-end report with photos. This ONE meeting > 30 emails.

30-60-90 Day Game Plan

Days 1-30: Establish Foundation

  • Set up reporting routine
  • Learn the technical work
  • Build tracking systems
  • Be 100% reliable

Goal: CEO sees consistency

Days 31-60: Add Value

  • Start flagging risks proactively
  • Present Month 1 report in person
  • Ask CEO for feedback
  • Improve based on feedback

Goal: CEO sees value beyond reporting

Days 61-90: Prove Indispensable

  • Propose one improvement idea
  • Show cost savings identified
  • Demonstrate technical growth
  • CEO can't imagine losing your visibility

Goal: CEO thinks "I need this person permanently"

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What if the contractors don't submit their daily logs on time?

Follow up immediately at 4:30 PM. Send a polite reminder: "Hi [Name], I need your daily log by 5 PM to include in tonight's CEO report. Can you send in the next 15 minutes?" Never let missing data slide. If it continues, mention it in your CEO report: "Note: Daily log was delayed by 2 hours, affecting report timeliness."

What if I don't understand the technical work the networking engineer is doing?

Ask them to explain! Say: "I need to document this for the CEO. Can you explain in simple terms what this configuration does and why it's important?" Take notes. Look it up later. Add it to your personal knowledge log. After 30 days of asking one question per day, you'll know 30 technical topics.

How detailed should my daily report be?

Keep it to 1 page. Be specific but concise. Instead of "networking work done today," write "Configured 12 ports on Switch-01, labeled cables A1-A12, tested connectivity to Server Room." Include 2-3 photos. Add one sentence on your quality check: "Work matches project plan, no issues noted."

What if something goes wrong on site?

Report it immediately, not at 6 PM. Call or message CEO right away: "FYI: Issue found with firewall configuration causing network outage. Engineer is resolving now. Will update in 30 minutes." Then include full details in evening report with: Issue → Impact → Resolution → Prevention. CEOs appreciate immediate bad news, not delayed bad news.

Should I report if a contractor is underperforming?

Yes, but factually. Don't say "Engineer is lazy." Say: "Planned: 8 tasks. Completed: 3 tasks. Reason cited: waiting for equipment delivery. Recommendation: Confirm equipment arrival time to avoid further delays tomorrow." Let facts speak. Your job is visibility, not judgment.

How do I prepare for the month-end presentation to CEO?

1) Print monthly report. 2) Prepare 10 before/after photos showing visible progress. 3) Create 3-5 PowerPoint slides: Summary → Network Progress → Sales Results → Risks → Next Month Plan. 4) Practice 15-minute presentation. 5) Be ready to answer: "What did we get for our money this month?" with specific deliverables.
